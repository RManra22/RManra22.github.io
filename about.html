About Me

Hello, like I mentioned before, I am a Mexican-American, first-generation college student. Currently, I am attending CSUF in pursuit of my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science. I plan to graduate from CSUF in the Spring of 2026, but there is still a chance that this could change. I also have my Associate degree in Computer Science from Santa Ana College. I hope to one day become a software engineer, or I would be happy with any job in tech. I fell in love with the tech world during the summer of my senior year. I had friends who were already coding, and by being around them, I also developed an interest in coding. This interest grew even more as I started watching tech-related videos on YouTube. My love for tech even led me to build my own gaming computer so I could play with those same friends. Over that summer, I knew that Computer Science was what I wanted to study in college. I even took a coding class in high school in hopes of preparing myself for college. Sadly, when it came time for college acceptances, I was not accepted into any of the Computer Science programs I applied to. Because of this, I decided to attend Santa Ana College in hopes of transferring to a four-year university after a few years. That is exactly what I did, and it has been the best decision I have made. Community college truly was the way to go since it was practically free and allowed me to get all the classes I considered boring out of the way. It was very tough at first because I worried a lot about the transfer process and how I would perform once at a four-year university.
